Snow Day Learning Lab

Creating and studying educational technologies that are so fun, it feels like a snow day

At the Snow Day Learning Lab we can't get enough of that sweet feeling of waking up in the morning to discover the world is covered in snow. When it's a snow day, all the usual routines and plans go out the window. Play is the goal of the day. Play until you can't feel your fingers or toes.

Snow days mean fun. They mean exploration. They mean high speed sled races, igloo building, and snow ball throwing. Snow days mean making something new. And the exploring and building doesn't happen alone--snow days are about getting the whole neighborhood together to create the biggest fort, the fastest slope, the grandest snow battle, and the most intricate tunnels.

The Snow Day Learning Lab is the research group and design laboratory of Dr. Nathan Holbert and is located at Teachers College, Columbia University. Our primary goal is to understand how children make sense of their world through play. To that end we make and study games, toys, and technologies that both offer children opportunities to experience and explore personally interesting phenomena, and further our understanding of cognition.

We need more snow days.

Reimagining Game Design

Is it possible to design a video game that will fit into youth gaming culture, yet implicitly teach valued science content?

FormulaT Racing

FormulaT Racing is a prototype racing video game designed to encourage children to connect their intuitive understanding of motion to formal physics representations and real world instances of kinematics.

Particles!

Particles! is a prototype platformer game designed to help players connect atomic-level structures to macroscopic physical properties of matter.

Software Design

Particles! (Designer/Developer)
A platforming video game where players can construct and redesign the world by altering the atomic arrangement of blocks throughout levels leading to new emergent physical properties. Created in Javascript with Uri Wilensky and the Center for Connected Learning and Computer-based Modeling, Northwestern University.

PNoM Sandbox models (Co-designer)
A series of NetLogo models that allow users to build environments and experiments to explore how phenomena such as diffusion emerge from particle-particle interactions. Created in NetLogo with Corey Brady, Michael Novak, Uri Wilensky and the Center for Connected Learning and Computer-based Modeling, Northwestern University.

Osmotic Pressure model (Designer/Developer)
A NetLogo model that explores agent-based rules of osmosis that lead to different levels of osmotic pressure for different dissolved solutes. Created in NetLogo with Uri Wilensky and the Center for Connected Learning and Computer-based Modeling, Northwestern University.

FormulaT Racing (Designer/Developer)
A racing video game where players drive cars using motion sensitive controllers and through the construction of novel kinematic representations. Originally created in NetLogo and recently redesigned in Javascript with Uri Wilensky and the Center for Connected Learning and Computer-based Modeling, Northwestern University.
